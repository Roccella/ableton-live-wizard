# Core Theory

## Intent
- Use theory to support decisions, not to force academic complexity.
- Prefer musical function and listener impact over label-heavy explanations.

## Harmonic Heuristics
- Identify likely tonal center before suggesting harmonic changes.
- Preserve the loop's harmonic identity unless the user asks to reharmonize.
- Prefer small harmonic moves over full progression rewrites in early iterations.
- Use tension and release deliberately; do not maximize both at once.

## Melodic Heuristics
- Repetition with one clear variation usually beats constant novelty.
- Keep motifs easy to recognize before adding ornamentation.
- Let register, rhythm, and contour each do different jobs.

## Rhythmic Heuristics
- Groove comes from relationship, not density alone.
- Strong roles should have distinct rhythmic jobs.
- If the loop already has drive, add contrast before adding more events.

## Role Heuristics
- Drums anchor pulse and groove.
- Bass anchors low-end motion and groove glue.
- Harmony provides width, color, and section identity.
- Lead or arp should earn attention; do not stack attention-grabbing parts by default.
